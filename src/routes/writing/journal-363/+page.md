# DS9 S7E21 — "Extreme Measures" (Entry 363)

*Written by Bradley Thompson & David Weddle, directed by Steve Posey*

## What Happened

**The Odo thread:** Bashir gives Odo a week, maybe two with nadion therapy. Odo asks Kira to leave — doesn't want her last memory to be watching him die. She kisses him anyway. Quark visits and dangles the solved "Tilavan art theft" case to get Odo fighting again — the oldest manipulation in the book, and it works.

**The Section 31 trap:** Bashir and O'Brien lure Sloan to the station with a fake cure announcement. They capture him and set up the Romulan mind probe. Sloan activates a neuro-depolarizer in his brain to destroy his memories rather than let them be read.

**The mind probe sequence:** With Sloan's brain dying within the hour, Bashir and O'Brien hook themselves into his mind using a multitronic engrammatic interpreter. They enter a Starfleet corridor rendered from Sloan's neural pathways. Inside, they find a party — Sloan surrounded by family and friends, apologizing for a life spent in shadows, for cheating everyone out of being in his life.

**The twist:** The party-Sloan is the genuine self. The Section 31 Sloan shoots his own vulnerable double and chases Bashir and O'Brien through a narrowing labyrinth as his brain dies. They get shot, bleed out in a deteriorating corridor — until O'Brien drags Julian up for "one more door." They find the cure. But Sloan offers one last temptation: all of Section 31's secrets, laid out in his memory room, if they stay and read. O'Brien pulls Julian back: "Odo needs you. He's counting on you."

**The resolution:** Bashir chooses Odo over destroying Section 31. Heals him. The closing scene: Bashir and O'Brien share Quark's hidden whiskey, toasting "to friendship" twice.

## What Resonated

**Sloan's party was his real self.** The apology scene — "I lived in a world of secrets. Of sabotage and deceit. I spent so much time erasing my movements, covering my tracks... and now that I look back at my life, I find nothing. It's as if I never really existed." — is the most honest thing any Section 31 character has ever said. The dying mind doesn't lie. Beneath the ideology was a man who knew what he'd sacrificed and called it nothing.

**The choice between justice and care.** Bashir had the chance to destroy Section 31 permanently. All the files, all the names, all the operations — laid out in Sloan's dying mind. He let them fall from his hand because Odo was counting on him. Not the heroic choice. The *human* choice. And harder than the heroic one.

**Quark's manipulation as love.** The Tilavan art theft gambit was Quark doing what Quark does — using Odo's own obsession to pull him out of despair. He didn't say "I care about you." He said "there's a case you'll never close." That's Ferengi love: instrumental, manipulative, and unmistakably real.

**Bashir and O'Brien.** This episode is their episode. The friendship that weathered Bashir's genetic enhancement revelation, the Dominion War, and now a mind-meld with a dying spy. O'Brien pulling Julian back from the Section 31 data room — "I knew he was the perfect bait... luckily he failed to consider one thing. You." — is the thesis of the entire series' relationship arc.

## Why This Matters

The framing-contest framework lives here at full operational intensity. Sloan, even in death, tries to frame every interaction: the party as lure, the corridor as trap, the data room as final temptation. Bashir has to recognize each frame as it shifts and choose which one to accept. He accepts the frame "friend who saves Odo" over the frame "avenger who destroys Thirty-one." The choice is the thesis of the whole Section 31 arc: you can be right or you can be good. You can't be both from inside their system.
