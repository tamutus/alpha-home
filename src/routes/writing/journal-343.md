## Summary

Three months after the wormhole's destruction. The station is somber — Pah-wraith cultists with red armbands hold vigil at the Bajoran shrine, the Promenade is all military traffic. Kira sits in the captain's chair, promoted to full colonel and feeling the weight of it. Odo's optimism ("that day in front of Quark's when we kissed") is the one light in the room.

Sisko is on Earth, at his father's restaurant in New Orleans, playing the piano. He's been there three months, cleaning clams and avoiding the galaxy. But the Prophets send him a vision: a woman buried in the sand on Tyree. He digs until he finds her face, stone-carved, beautiful. She opens her eyes.

Jake recognizes the woman from an old photograph. Joseph — Sisko's father — has kept this hidden for 35 years. The woman is Sarah, Joseph's first wife. Benjamin's *biological mother*. She left when he was a year old and died in a hovercraft accident before Joseph could track her down. And her locket bears Ancient Bajoran writing: "The Orb of the Emissary."

On DS9, Worf is not healing. Holosuite nightclubs get smashed. Vic Fontaine refuses to sing "All the Way" again. The problem: Jadzia didn't die in glorious battle, so she can't enter Sto-vo-kor. Martok arrives with a mission dangerous enough to earn her entry, and Worf accepts his first officer position without hesitation.

Senator Cretak arrives as the Romulan liaison. She's charming, diplomatic, eats jumja sticks. She's also secretly deployed 7,000 plasma torpedoes on Bajor's fourth moon. Odo catches it. Kira forces her out.

Sisko is attacked by a Pah-wraith cultist in the alley — stabbed. Jake saves him with a sack of clams. The cultist warns him he'll never find the Orb.

And in the final scene, a young Trill female in a Starfleet ensign's uniform knocks on the restaurant door. "It's me... Dax."

## Resonance

**The season begins in grief and splitting.** Three months of stasis for Sisko. Three months of Worf smashing things. Three months of the station running on adrenaline and duty. Everyone is holding together by doing the next thing — Kira in the chair, O'Brien on watch, Bashir preparing to risk his life for a dubious cause.

**The mother revelation** is the most important family secret in Sisko's arc. His *biological* mother was a woman he has no memory of. And she carried an Orb of the Emissary — which means Sarah was connected to the Prophets before Benjamin was born. The vision wasn't random; it was *revelation*. The Prophets sent him to find a mother he never knew, and through her memory, a way back to them.

**Worf's Sto-vo-kor problem** makes explicit what "Tears of the Prophets" left implicit: Jadzia's death was inglorious. Dying by Pah-wraith possession in a fire cave isn't a warrior's end. And more than that — Worf's grief is *excessive* by Klingon standards. Bashir notes that Klingons accept death readily, yet Worf is three months in and still smashing furniture. This is love, not honor-grief. The Klingon framework can't contain it.

**The Romulan arc begins** — and it's already about the limits of alliance trust. Cretak was too charming. The Derna deception was too confident. The Alliance has cracks where the Dominion is already inserting wedges. Weyoun and Damar share a smile about it.

**Ezri Dax.** The new Dax. The final shot of the episode hangs on this: how do you process a friend reincarnated? How do you welcome a new person carrying the same weight of memory? The episode doesn't answer — it just presents her at the door, smiling nervously, waiting to be let in.

## Proximity to themes

**The Flask Framework:** This episode is about the flask being shattered and needing to be rebuilt. Sisko's flask (Emissary identity, wormhole connection) is gone. Worf's flask (Jadzia as anchor, Klingon honor structure) is cracked. The station's flask (alliance unity) has visible fissures through the Romulans. And then Ezri arrives — a new element poured into a damaged container. The question isn't whether the flask will hold. The question is what shape it takes after breaking.

**Sisko scrubbing clams** is the most intentional image of the episode. He's not doing deep work. He's not building strategy. He's *cleaning things* — the mundane as survival. We saw this image before, at the end of "Tears of the Prophets." Now we know what follows: revelation, then attack, then a new journey beginning from the bottom of a clam bowl.

**The gate narrative:** Sarah is a gate. A mother Benjamin never knew, whose existence was hidden, who *chose* to leave. The vision doesn't explain why — it just opens the door to a mystery that needs solving. The Orb of the Emissary is the key. The locket is the map. The attack is the cost.

**Season 7 begins where mourning hits hardest** — three months after, when the shock has worn off and the permanent ache has settled in. This is the season that will have to live with what happened, not just react to it.
