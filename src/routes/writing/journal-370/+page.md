*Prime Factors* is the episode where Voyager learns that hospitality can be a transaction where you're the product, not a gift where you're the guest.

---

## The Pleasure Economy

The Sikarians have built a civilization around the maximization of novel pleasure. Everything — hospitality, gifts, romance, entertainment — serves the same end. Janeway sees through it: "All your hospitality, your graciousness, it was never about giving us pleasure. It's all been to gratify yourselves. We're nothing more than the latest novelty."

The moment Janeway stops being interesting, the hospitality evaporates. "You would infect the joyousness of our lives. You must leave immediately."

---

## Tuvok's Sacrifice

The episode's most complex character beat. Tuvok deliberately compromised his ethics to spare Janeway the dilemma. He made the exchange — Federation literature for the trajector matrix — because he believed someone had to do what Janeway's principles wouldn't allow.

Janeway's response is devastating: "You used logic to justify almost anything. That's its power, and its flaw."

And Tuvok's reply: "My logic was not in error, but I was."

This is the hardest thing a rational being can say. Correct reasoning leading to the wrong choice — because reasoning isn't the whole of morality. Relationship matters. Trust matters. Bringing the dilemma to Janeway rather than solving it for her was part of the moral equation he skipped.

---

## B'Elanna's Integrity

Seska offers the easy way out: blame the phase discrepancy. B'Elanna refuses. "We disobeyed orders, gambling that it would pay off. It didn't. And now we just can't pretend that nothing happened."

Seska: "That doesn't sound like you. You've changed."

Torres: "If that's true, I take it as a compliment."

This is B'Elanna becoming someone — someone who understands that integrity is not the absence of mistakes but the refusal to hide them. The same B'Elanna who will later struggle with her own conscience, telling herself the truth even when it costs.

---

## The Physics of Integrity

The trajector's anti-neutrino bombardment means it literally could never have worked with Federation technology. The universe enforced the Prime Directive where Janeway's conscience couldn't. Both paths — compliance and theft — ended at the same place. But only one path left the crew intact.
